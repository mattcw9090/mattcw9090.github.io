MyFinanceTracker

MyFinanceTracker is an iOS app built entirely with SwiftUI, leveraging Core Data for data persistence and iCloud for seamless syncing. The app helps users manage their finances by tracking transactions, net income, and outstanding debts or credits. It follows an MVVM architecture, making the code cleaner, more organized, and easier to maintain.

Key Features

  • Real-Time Expense Tracking: Stay updated on day-to-day expenses and incomes. (See NetIncomeTabView and AddTransactionView)
  • Budget Management & Alerts: Keep an eye on your net income and get alerted if things go off track.
  • Data Visualization with Charts & Graphs: Planned for future iterations to make expenses more intuitive.
  • Secure Storage with Core Data & iCloud Sync: All financial data is stored locally using NSPersistentContainer while also syncing with iCloud. (See PersistenceController)

Technical Highlights

  • SwiftUI & MVVM Architecture: Modern UI with separated state management for maintainable code.
  • Core Data Integration: Persistent storage for transactions and custom entities like CashFlowItem, PredefinedTransaction, and QuickAddTransaction.
  • EnvironmentObjects & Publishers: NetIncomeManager is an ObservableObject that publishes changes to net income across the app, enabling real-time updates. (See NetIncomeManager.swift)
  • Tabbed Navigation & Modals: Multi-tab layout for net income, cash flow, and settings, each with custom modals for adding or editing data. (See ContentView.swift and its child views)

How It Works

The entry point, MyFinanceTrackerApp, initializes a shared PersistenceController and a NetIncomeManager. Once inside the app, you’ll find three main tabs in ContentView.swift:

  • Net Income Tab (via NetIncomeTabView) – Displays net income, allows adding or editing transactions by day, and automatically adjusts net income through NetIncomeManager.
  • Cash Flow Tab (via CashFlowTabView) – Lists what you owe and who owes you. Tapping a cash flow item opens up an EditCashFlowItemView for modifications.
  • Settings Tab (via SettingsTabView) – Houses predefined transactions and quick-add transactions, making it easy to initialize or quickly log frequent expenses/income.

Every time you add, edit, or delete a transaction, NetIncomeManager updates the net income accordingly (see adjustNetIncome method). The data model is defined in MyFinanceTracker.xcdatamodeld, where you’ll find entities for storing Transactions, CashFlowItems, and more.
